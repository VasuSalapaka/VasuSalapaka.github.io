  Here we are developing frameworks for designing algorithms to efficiently solve combinatorial optimization problems that arise in data/network classification and aggregation, resource allocation, routing, and sequential decision making problems. Many of these problems have non convex cost functions that are riddled with multiple local minima, and are proved to be computationally complex (NP-hard). We are developing a comprehensive framework where we address the combinatorial computational complexity, variability in associated clustering or coverage cost functions, area-specific constraints on the network structure, communication, and dynamics of constituent elements and their interactions or interdependencies. The central idea of our framework is based on maximum entropy principle (MEP) from statistics and minimum free energy principle from thermodynamics (it mimics statistical mechanical solutions to similar combinatorial problems that occur in nature).
